What a data product contains.

Seven clauses, one shape — every governed data product carries the same template, whether source-aligned or aggregated.

01 Informal data — today's baseline Today
No formal spec. Data exists as a byproduct: a table, an export, owned implicitly by whoever built it. Schema, contracts, quality, and governance live in tribal knowledge — if at all. Most Laerdal data starts here.

Data product classification

Every data product is classified by how consumers expect to use it. The Session data product is an Enabler. Separate from classification, each product also has a product type (source-aligned, aggregated, etc.) describing how it is built — see §1 Identity.

ClassificationUse caseMarketSuccess measures
Utility When a consumer expects a product to be instantly available, for all purposes Internal or external Product awareness, availability and time to access
Enabler When a consumer expects a product to enable decision making for specific business outcomes Internal or external Time or money saved in achieving a specific business outcome
Driver When the product is expected to drive the business Typically focused on external monetisation by selling for economic value Revenue generated
