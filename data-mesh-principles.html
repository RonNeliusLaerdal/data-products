Data & AI strategy · the way forward

Data you can trust, by design.

Data mesh: four principles that make every product owned, documented, and built to be used — defined by Zhamak Dehghani (2019).

The four principles — and how they show up at Laerdal
01 · Domain-oriented ownership
Owned by the domain that creates value from it.
It sits with the business domain, not a central data team — accountable for what the data means and the value it creates.
At Laerdal
A source domain owns what it generates; an aggregated domain owns the canonical definition every producer adheres to.
02 · Data as a product
Spotlight
Data is designed and built for its consumers.
Data is designed, built, and maintained for its consumers — discoverable, trustworthy, documented, and secure.
At Laerdal
Every product is built for the people, and the AI, that will use it — the six qualities below set the bar.
03 · Self-serve data platform
A shared platform lowers the barrier.
A shared platform lets domain teams build, share, and consume data products on their own.
At Laerdal
One Data Backbone — Databricks, Unity Catalog, Unified Events — so any domain can publish without building the plumbing.
04 · Federated (computational) governance
Global rules, automated and embedded.
Global rules are automated and embedded in the platform — not enforced by a central gatekeeper.
At Laerdal
Standards set once by the Data Governance Hub, then enforced automatically — so governance is a service, not a gatekeeper.
02 · Data as a product
Treat data like a product we ship — not a byproduct we leave behind.
01
Has a clear owner
Someone is accountable — like a product manager for the data.
02
Easy to find & understand
Named, described, and discoverable — not hidden in someone's spreadsheet.
03
Comes with a promise
A contract: what it contains, how often it updates, what quality to expect.
04
Trusted & secure
Accurate, compliant, and only accessible to those who should see it.
05
Built for consumers
Designed around who will use it — reporting, apps, partners, AI.
06
Maintained over time
Versioned, improved, and supported — not a one-off extract.
